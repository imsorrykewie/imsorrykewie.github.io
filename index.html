• To kewie

A silly little project for my best friend,
the closest one I had

Dear kewie,

Before I say anything else, I just want to thank you for all the things we were able to do and the days we were able to spend together. They were truly amazing, and I wish each day with you could have lasted longer.
I'm glad I met you.
It's been two months, and life hasn't been the same since.
I've never been too artistic, so I don't know how to best express my feelings and ended up doing this.
I've also never made an actual website before, I'm sorry if it sucks. It turns out you need to learn three programming languages just to make a simple website.
I've been suffering. It feels like I'm being stabbed in the chest every day.
But it's okay.
You were always kind and cared for me, so I'm sure you didn't mean to hurt me this much when you left.
I hope you were able to be happy this whole time. At least then I can find some meaning in my pain.
Making this helped me let out some of my emotions and distract me.
It made me feel like I was talking with you again, when things were simple and nice.
Kewie...
I'm really doing everything I can, so please reconsider.
I've really grown a lot, and I wish we could be a part of each other's stories again.

But... Why?

We've never actually met,
never seen how we look like,
never heard each other's voices.

You're probably wondering why I'm going through all of this effort.
At first I didn't know why I was doing this either. I didn't know how I grew to be so attached to this friendship, or how I talked with you every day, or how I ended up spending some of my most precious moments with you.
I didn't know why you left so abruptly, and tossed away everything without wanting to talk at all.
Everything was a blur.
I have spent the past few months searching for answers, collecting our memories, looking at what happened between us and where it all went wrong.

I'm trying so hard because I want to show how much you mattered to me.
I'm sure you didn't want everything to turn out this way either, with everything we've been through together.
I'm sorry for ruining everything.
But you were a vital part of my life the past two years, and if there is anything I could do to continue our friendship, I wouldn't hesitate for a single moment.
I want to show you how much effort I am willing to make to improve myself,
to get back with you and make sure you always feel respected as a person and as a dear friend.
I really want to make this work again, so please believe in me.
I keep my promises.
I guess I also don't want you to leave thinking I'm a bad person.
I want to hold on to the hope that you could give this friendship another chance.
I've been writing down my daily stories,
wondering if I could finally share them with you again someday.

I made this in the hopes that you could understand me, and why I sometimes behaved the way I did,
which caused tensions in our friendship and ultimately made you leave.
I hope you can journey through my story, or at least enjoy my playlist while scrolling through, because it took me a while to figure out how to make everything.
I miss you.
Wherever you are.

My Life Before

Having no siblings, I've always been faced with some high expectations from family.
They were nice, but also strict.
I also moved a lot, so I never had a typical childhood or a childhood friend.
No pony with misspelled cake or trips to places that don't exist on the map.
Throughout elementary and middle school, I kept up the image of a good student, even though nothing really mattered back then. People ended up having expectations of me that I never wanted.
I was always stressed and overworking myself.

I coped in some pretty unhealthy ways. I stopped going out as much and stayed up late every night.
I let my emotions build up and left all the issues in life for future me.
I did have a few friends, but never someone I could open up to.
I wanted people to know me better.
Because if they did,
then maybe they could understand my problems.
And I wouldn't feel so lonely anymore.

I turned to video games for my loneliness. Meeting random people online was better than having nobody at all.
It worked at first, I could immerse myself, talk to people I've never met, and distract myself from the world.
I didn't have to conform to any expectations or face any responsibilities.
But that never really solved the problem, and I knew inside that internet strangers would never be able to truly understand me.
...Or at least that's what I thought.

Until I Met You

Our encounter was purely by chance. I never expected our friendship to grow so fast.
Talking to you brought me joy like never before.
I still remember us staying up talking about all the nerdy topics until late at night, or casually chatting about our lives while doing nothing in particular.
I don't really know what happened to casper, we kind of just stopped talking, but I loved our time together too.
I enjoyed our conversations so much that I was playing the game just to talk with you.
You even took the time to log on just to say happy birthday to me, when even a lot of my friends forgot.
Thank you.

I don't know exactly how either, but I felt like I could open up to you. Maybe because I didn't have to be scared of showing my insecurities to the people see every day or worry about being judged.
And then we got discord and had over 1,000 messages in the first day.
I've always wanted an online best friend. This became more than I could have ever asked for.
We were inseparable, and for once, everything in life was just wonderful.
I had someone to accompany me throughout the day, someone to share happy moments with, someone to turn to when I'm down, someone I could connect to. I no longer had to bottle up all my emotions.
When I was talking with you, I felt genuinely happy.
I really loved our moments together and appreciated all the things you were able to do with me.
There was finally someone who could understand me.
I wished we could stay this way forever.

But I know it wasn't always perfect.
At first, we had small fights every few weeks.
It was always tough, but in the end I felt we gained a deeper understanding of each other.
I really tried listening to your concerns and changing myself. There were many times when I wanted to do things with you, but decided not to force it because you were busy or had other things.
I guess I'm a bit insecure about missing out on things.
But our fights became less and less frequent, and we were able to address the problems when we did.
They turned from every few weeks to a month to every other month. Our last ones were 3 months apart.
So I just hope you can see that I really was trying.

But I couldn't change fast enough.

When You Left

I didn't expect you to say goodbye so suddenly. At the time, I thought our friendship was pretty solid and stable.
I thought you were enjoying it as much as I did, but looking back, there were cracks I couldn't notice.
We hadn't done anything for a month, and at the time I was also going through some really stressful things.
College deadlines were closing in and some classes weren't ideal, as well as things going on with family too.
I felt that we were drifting apart from each other. It was as if things were turning back to before I met you.
It was a time when I really needed you, so when I found out that I slept through the little window of time when you were finally able to do things...

I'm sorry.

I did and said things I should not have.
I just really, really wanted to finally spend some time with you again. I hope you can understand.
But that doesn't mean my behavior was justified. I made you uncomfortable and crossed the line.
I'm sorry I treated you as just someone behind a screen to play games and watch shows with.
I failed to consider your feelings and failed as a friend.
I'm really, really, really sorry.
Right before you left, you said you didn't feel like you and your boundaries were being respected.
Those words echo inside me every day.
I'm sorry I wasn't able to provide you with a friendship you could be comfortable in, unlike what you did for me.
I wish we would have communicated more, maybe none of this would have happened. Maybe we would be enjoying our breaks doing all the things we loved together.
Maybe.
But sadly time doesn't go back.

I coped in some destructive ways.
I made everyone worry.
I questioned everything.
I realized I had it all wrong.

Thanks to some help, I've gotten back up a little.
But it has been a painful journey without you.
I've been looking through our memories every day, finding fragments of our friendship everywhere I go.
Everything feels hollow now.
I just want to talk like we used to.
I want to be there for you like you did for me.
I really hope you can see how much effort I'm willing to put in to make things better.
I promise I'll do everything I can to respect your boundaries and listen to your concerns. So please give me another chance.
I don't want to keep convincing myself that this relationship should have ended then.
I don't want to pretend that it was all a fantasy.
Because every moment with you mattered to me,
and I'm willing to do whatever it takes to change things from the way it is now.

Let's rebuild the friendship that we loved,
and turn it into something we've always wanted it to be.
Together.
I promise you won't regret it.

Made with love, memories, and tears by a past bestie.
