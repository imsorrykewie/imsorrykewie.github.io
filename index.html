• To kewie

A silly little project for my best friend,
the closest one I had

Dear kewie,

I've never been too artistic, so I don't know how to best express my feelings.
I ended up doing this. I've never made an actual website before, so I'm sorry if it sucks.
Turns out you need to learn three programming languages just to make a simple website
I've been suffering. Feels like I've been stabbed in the chest every time I see our memories.
But it's okay.
You were always kind and cared for me, so I'm sure you didn't mean to hurt me this much when you left.
Making this helped me let out some of my emotions and distract me from the pain.
It made me feel like I was talking with you again.

But... Why?

You're probably wondering why I'm going through this much effort.
We've never met,
never seen how we look like,
never heard each other's voices.
Well, at first I didn't really know why I was doing this either.
I didn't know how I grew to be so attached to this friendship, or how I talked with you every day, or how I ended up spending some of my most precious moments with you.
I didn't know why you left so abruptly, and tossed away everything without wanting to talk at all.
Everything was a blur.
I have spent the past few months searching for answers,
looking at what happened between us and where it all went wrong.
I'm trying so hard because I want to show how much you mattered to me.
If there was anything I could do to be friends again, I wouldn't hesitate for a moment.
I want to show you how much effort I am willing to make to improve myself,
and to get back with you, and make sure you always feel respected as a person and as a dear friend.
I guess I just don't want you to leave thinking I'm a bad person,
and maybe hold on to the hope that you could give this friendship another chance.
I made this in the hopes that you could understand me, and why I sometimes behaved the way I did,
which caused tensions in our friendship and ultimately made you leave.
I hope you can find the time, enjoy the songs, and journey through my story.

My Life Before

Having no siblings, I've always been faced with some high expectations from family.
They were nice, but also strict.
Throughout elementary and middle school, I tried keeping up the image of a good student, even though nothing really mattered back then. People ended up having expectations of me that I never wanted.
I was always stressed and overworking myself.

I coped in some pretty unhealthy ways. I stopped going out as much and stayed up late every night.
I let my emotions build up and left all the issues in life for future me.
I did have a few friends,
but never someone I could open up to.
I wanted people to know me better.
Because if they did,
then maybe they could understand my problems.
And I wouldn't feel so lonely.

I turned to online games for my loneliness. Meeting random people online was better than having nobody at all.
It somewhat at first, I could talk to people I've never met and distract myself from the world.
I didn't have to conform to any expectations or face any responsibilities at all.
But that never really solved the problem, and I knew inside that internet strangers would never be able to truly understand me.
...Or at least that's what I thought.

Until I Met You

It was purely by chance that I met you. I never expected our friendship to grow so fast.
Talking to you brought me joy like never before.
I still remember us staying up talking about nerdy topics until late at night, or casually chatting about our lives while doing nothing in particular.
I don't really know what happened to casper, we kind of just stopped talking, but I loved our time together too.
I enjoyed our conversations so much that I was playing the game just to talk with you.
You even took the time to log on just to say happy birthday to me.
Thank you.

I don't really know exactly how either, but I felt like I could open up to you and really trusted you.
Maybe because I didn't have to be scared of showing my insecurities to people I see every day and being judged.
And then we got discord and had over 1,000 messages in the first day. It was really amazing.
I had someone to accompany me throughout the day, someone to share happy moments with, someone to turn to when I'm down, someone I could connect to. I no longer had to bottle up all my emotions.
When I was talking with you, I felt genuinely happy.
I really loved our moments together and appreciated all the things you were able to do with me.
I had found someone who could understand me.
I hoped we could stay this way forever.

But I know it wasn't always perfect.
At first, we had fights every few weeks.
It was always tough, but in the end I felt we gained a deeper understanding into each other.
I really tried listening to your concerns and changing myself. There were many times when I wanted to do things with you, but decided not to force it because you were busy or had other things.
Our fights became less and less frequent, and we were able to address the problems when we did.
They turned from every few weeks to a month to every other month. Our last ones were over 3 months apart.
So I just hope you can see that I really was trying.

But I guess I couldn't change fast enough.

When You Left

I guess I just didn't expect you to say goodbye so suddenly.
At the time, I thought our friendship was pretty solid and stable.
I thought you were enjoying it as much as I did, but looking back I guess there were cracks I couldn't notice.
We hadn't done anything for a month, and at the time I was also going through some really stressful things.
College deadlines were closing in and some classes weren't ideal.
I felt as if things were turning back to before I met you, and that we were drifting apart from each other.
It was a time when I really needed you, so when I found out that I slept through the little window of time when you were finally free...

I'm sorry.

I did and said things I should not have.
I made you feel uncomfortable and crossed the line.
Right before you left, you said you didn't feel like you and your boundaries were being respected.
Those words echo inside me every day.
I'm sorry I wasn't able to provide you with a friendship you could be comfortable in, unlike what you did for me.
I wish we would have communicated more, maybe none of this would have happened and we would be enjoying our breaks doing all the things we loved together.
But sadly time doesn't go back.

I coped in some destructive ways.
I made everyone worry.
I questioned the meaning of everything.
I realized I had it all wrong.

Thanks to some help, I've pulled myself back a little.
But it has been a painful journey without you.
I've been looking through our memories every day.
I've been finding fragments of our relationship in everything I do.
I just want to talk like we used to.
I want to be there for you like you did for me.
I really hope you can see how much effort I'm willing to put in to make things better.
I promise I'll do everything I can to respect your boundaries and listen to your concerns. So please give me another chance.
I don't want to keep convincing myself that this relationship should have ended then.
I don't want to pretend that it was all a fantasy.
Because every moment with you mattered to me,
and I'm willing to do anything to change things from the way it is now.

Let's mend the friendship that we loved,
and turn it into something we've always wanted it to be.
Together.

Made with love, memories, and tears by a past bestie.
